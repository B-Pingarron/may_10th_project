In Memory of Palestine's Mothers and Children

This Mother's Day, we bear witness to the 60,199 identified lives lost in Gaza — a number that represents not a final count but a floor. Among the dead are 18,457 children, including 968 infants under one year old. An estimated 10,000+ additional people lie buried under rubble, their names still unrecorded. In a territory of 2.3 million people, this is a loss at a scale and rate that the 21st century has rarely seen. Every statistic is a stolen future — a mother who will never hold her child again, a child who will never know their mother's love.

Key Numbers — Gaza Death Toll

The Gaza Ministry of Health, in coordination with the Palestinian Ministry of Health in Ramallah and hospital directors across the Gaza Strip, has identified 60,199 individuals killed since the escalation of hostilities. This is the most detailed, granular count of any conflict zone in the world.

Healthcare Under Attack

Gaza's healthcare system has been systematically dismantled. Of 36 hospitals operating before the conflict, only 10 (28%) remain functional — and those at severely reduced capacity. 1,722 healthcare workers have been killed, crippling the system's ability to respond to the overwhelming number of casualties.

MoH vs. Lancet — 34.7% Higher

The 60,199 identified victims documented by the Gaza Ministry of Health represents a verified, name-by-name count. However, a Lancet study published in January 2025 estimated the true number of excess violent deaths at 75,200+ — a figure 34.7% higher. The discrepancy is largely driven by an estimated 10,000+ people still buried under rubble who have not been recovered or identified.

Methodological note: The Lancet estimate uses statistical modeling based on multiple data sources including hospital reports, burial records, media reports, and morgue counts. The 10,000+ missing under rubble represent victims who died but whose bodies could not be recovered due to ongoing hostilities, lack of heavy equipment, and the destruction of civil defense infrastructure. The true toll likely falls somewhere between these two figures.

Data Sources & Methodology

Data Collection: The Gaza Ministry of Health, in coordination with the Palestinian Ministry of Health in Ramallah, maintains a detailed registry of all identified victims. Each entry includes name, age, sex, and ID number, cross-referenced with hospital admission records, burial permits, and family testimonies.

Confidence Levels:

  • Verified — Confirmed by multiple authoritative sources including UN, WHO, and independent researchers
  • Reported — Reported by reliable sources but requiring additional cross-verification
  • Estimated — Statistical estimates based on available data and modeling
